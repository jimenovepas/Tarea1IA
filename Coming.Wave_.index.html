Containment Is Not Possible

by Jimena Novegil Pastoriza

Prologue: "Containment Is Not Possible"

Essence: The book examines whether it is possible to contain the current technological wave and its implications.

Justification: From where we stand today, containing this wave—controlling, curbing, or stopping it—is not possible.

Chapter 1: "Containment Is Not Possible"

Essence: Technological containment is essential, but there's no clear plan to achieve it.

Justification: We urgently need answers for how the coming wave can be controlled and contained.

Chapter 2: "Endless Proliferation"

Essence: The difficulty of containment lies in the historical and cumulative nature of technological waves.

Justification: Understand these waves, and the challenge of containment becomes clear.

Chapter 3: "The Containment Problem"

Essence: Applying Cold War strategies of containment to technology presents unique challenges.

Justification: For many, "containment" echoes the Cold War.

Chapter 4: "The Technology of Intelligence"

Essence: Intelligence is the ability to predict future scenarios, crucial for understanding AI's impact.

Justification: Intelligence can be understood as predicting plausible scenarios about the world.

Chapter 5: "The Technology of Life"

Essence: The revolution in biology, including genetic engineering, presents containment challenges.

Justification: Everything from agriculture to medicine will depend on biology fundamentals.

Chapter 6: "The Wider Wave"

Essence: Technologies like robotics and quantum computing will intersect in complex ways.

Justification: Associated technologies will grow and intersect turbulently.

Chapter 7: "Four Features of the Coming Wave"

Essence: The wave is omni-use, hyper-evolving, asymmetric, and autonomous, making containment difficult.

Justification: These features explain why this isn't business as usual.

Chapter 8: "Unstoppable Incentives"

Essence: Economic, political, and security incentives drive technological development.

Justification: No one can wholly govern these entrenched incentives.

Chapter 9: "The Grand Bargain"

Essence: The contract between the State and its citizens is threatened by the technological wave.

Justification: The nation-state offers a simple bargain to its citizens.

Chapter 10: "Fragility Amplifiers"

Essence: New technologies exacerbate vulnerabilities, increasing fragility.

Justification: Applications of coming-wave technologies will shake brittle foundations.

Chapter 11: "The Future of Nations"

Essence: The technological wave could transform nations, centralizing or decentralizing power.

Justification: Some states may become zombie governments.

Chapter 12: "The Dilemma"

Essence: A dilemma exists between potential benefits and existential risks.

Justification: We must grapple with good and bad outcomes ignited by openness.

Chapter 13: "Containment Must Be Possible"

Essence: Regulation alone is insufficient for containment.

Justification: Containment is not possible, yet it must be.

Chapter 14: "Ten Steps Toward Containment"

Essence: Ten concrete steps toward containment are proposed.

Justification: This is groundwork, not final answers.
